**1. The "Heart-Healthy" Diet**

- **Focus:** Switch to a Mediterranean-style diet (high in healthy fats like olive oil, fish, nuts, and lots of fiber/vegetables).
- **The Goal:** Lowering "bad" LDL cholesterol can stop plaque from growing and, over time, help stabilize it so it's less likely to cause issues.

**2. Stress Management**

- Since arguments trigger this, learning "de-escalation" or breathing techniques isn't just for your marriage—it's for your heart. When you feel that heat rising during a disagreement, your heart is literally under physical strain.

**3. Controlled Exercise**

- Don't stop moving, but **change how you move.** A doctor might suggest "Zone 2" cardio (lower intensity) where your heart rate stays below the "pain threshold" while you build cardiovascular efficiency.

**4. Medication (Medical Intervention)**

- Doctors often prescribe **Beta-blockers** (which keep the heart rate from spiking too high during stress/exercise) or **Statins** (to manage cholesterol).

**The "Bottom Line"**

If your heart is sending you signals during both physical and **emotional** stress, your body is telling you that its "safety margin" is thin. The good news is that catching this while it is "stable" (predictable) is the best-case scenario for long-term health. It means you have the chance to address it before it becomes an "unstable" or emergency situation.

**Your Next Step**

**Schedule a "Stress Test" or a "Nuclear Stress Test."** This is the gold standard for your situation. They will put you on a treadmill and monitor your heart while it's under stress to see exactly what is happening to the blood flow.
