1. **Short words. No debates.**

At 20 months, talking more = pouring gas on it.

“Sturdy Pivot” - When a plane hits turbulence, the pilot doesn't scream or give in to the passengers' demands; they stay calm and fly the plane.

- **Stop Negotiating:** He is 20 months old; he cannot be reasoned with during a tantrum.
- **Don't Get Angry:** Your anger tells him that his big emotions are scary enough to "break" you. When you stay calm, you show him that you are strong enough to handle his big feelings.

**2. The "Acknowledge and Hold" Method**

This is the core technique for when you say "no" and the crying starts. Instead of trying to stop the crying, **allow the crying while maintaining the boundary.**

- **The Boundary:** "No, you cannot have another cookie."
- **The Validation:** "I hear you. You really wanted that cookie. It’s hard to hear no."
- **The Consistency:** Let him cry. Don't try to distract him with a toy or shush him. Just stay near him so he knows he’s safe, but **do not give in**. If you give in once, you teach him that the "fit" is the key to getting what he wants.
