- **Formal Demand Letter:** If not already done recently, a strong, updated demand letter outlining the case, damages, and a deadline for response might be needed.
- **Filing a Lawsuit (if not already done):** Often, insurance companies don't get serious until a lawsuit is actually filed. If you haven't filed suit yet, ask your lawyer why not and if it's time. If you *have* filed, ask what the next step in the litigation process is and why it's stalled.
- **Motion to Compel:** If the delay is due to the insurance company not providing requested information (discovery), your lawyer can file a motion to compel them to respond.
- **<mark style="background:#d3f8b6">Complaint to the Department of Insurance:** Your lawyer can advise if filing a formal complaint with the Texas Department of Insurance against the insurer for unreasonable delays is warranted. This can sometimes light a fire under them.</mark>
