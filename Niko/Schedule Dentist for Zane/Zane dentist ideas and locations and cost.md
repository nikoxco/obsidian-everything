brushing teeth: 

- Let Him Choose: Give him some say in the process. Let him pick out a toothbrush and a toothpaste. There are a ton of fun kids' toothbrushes and milder-flavored toothpastes out there that aren't minty.
- Give Him a Job: Let him hold one toothbrush while you use a second one to do the actual cleaning. This gives him something to do and makes him feel like he's in control. You could also try the "your turn, my turn" approach.
- Find a Comfy Position: Try different positions. It might be easier for him if he's sitting on your lap with his head resting on your body.
- Stay Consistent: Even if it's a total fight, keep at it. Making it a consistent part of the daily routine is what's most important, and eventually, he'll get used to it.

Making the Dentist Fun
The first visit is huge for setting the tone. You want to make it feel like an adventure, not a scary appointment.

- Bring a Comfort Item: Let him bring his favorite stuffed animal or blanket. Having something familiar can make a new place feel a lot less intimidating.
- Offer a Reward: After the visit, you can have a special outing or get a small toy to celebrate his bravery. Just avoid rewarding him with candy.


Out of Pocket Costs
This part is a little tricky because it depends on a lot of factors, like if you have insurance and what's covered.

- Average Cost: For a basic cleaning and fluoride application, the average out-of-pocket cost can be anywhere from $30-$60 without insurance. A basic cleaning and polish can run from $75 to $200.
- New Patient Specials: Some places, like Austin Children's Dentistry, offer new patient specials for around $79, which can include an exam, x-rays, cleaning, and a fluoride treatment. This could be a good way to save money on the first visit.
- Call Ahead: The best thing to do is call the office you're interested in and ask about their pricing and what they cover with your specific insurance. They should be able to give you a clear breakdown of what you'll be responsible for.
